Description

Chole bhature is a North Indian dish pairing chickpea curry with bhatura, a deep-fried flatbread. A common street food, chole bhature is often eaten as a breakfast dish. It is associated with Punjabi cuisine, though various views exist on the dish's origin.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • ⅓ cup sooji (rava or fine semolina)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 tablespoon granulated fine sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt or as required
  • ¼ teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ cup curd (yogurt)
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons water for kneading or add as needed
  • oil for deep frying

Steps

  1. First, rinse 1 cup white chickpeas a couple of times in water. Then soak them in enough water to cover them for 7-8 hours or overnight.
  2. In a pressure cooker, add soaked chickpeas, dry amla, dry red chilli, black cardamom, salt to taste, baking soda, bay leaf, tea powder and water as required.
  3. Cover and pressure cook for 5 whistles. If you do not have tea bags, then you can prepare tea decoction and add it to the cooker.
  4. In a bowl, add Black cardamom, Black peppercorns, Green cardamom, Mace, Dry fenugreek leaves.
  5. Add Cinnamon stick, Nutmeg, Star anise, Cloves, Fenugreek seeds.
  6. Transfer this into the pan and dry roast until the spices turn aromatic.
  7. In a kadai, heat ghee, add pressure cooked chole, prepared chole masala, black tamarind water.
  8. AMix everything well to make sure the chole doesn’t break.
  9. Adjust consistency by adding remaining chole water.
  10. ASimmer for 15-20 minutes on low flame, or until chole absorbs all the flavour.
  11. At the end, add ginger and ghee.
  12. Garnished with ginger, ghee, green chillies, fried aloo, sliced onion and coriander sprig.
  13. In a parat, add refined flour, sugar, salt to taste, oil, dry yeast soaked in water, semolina soaked in water for 1 hour and mix well making sure everything is well combined.
  14. Further, add water as required and knead the dough
  15. Pinch a ball sized dough and make a ball without any cracks.
  16. Finally, roll the dough evenly into circles using a rolling pin. roll neither too thin or thick. Heat oil in a deep kadai. When the oil is sufficiently hot, add one bhatura.
  17. Flip over and fry the bhatura till golden brown all over.
  18. Then drain the bhature into tissue paper to remove excess oil. Fry all bhature the same way..
  19. Serve hot with fried bhature and green chutney.
